FROM KIDS TO CODE

Though my career has been people focused, I have always had a "techie" side. After working with urban youth for over 20 years I had the opportunity to move into a position that enabled me to develop these interests more. I found myself learning so many new things which brought a renewed energy and a change in focus to my career path. Below are some examples of my recent work that will demonstrate my background further and serve as milestones on my journey "from kids to code".

Database Project

Project Summary: Create a customized database application that integrated students from all 8 programs and track key metrics used in reporting to stakeholders and in guiding department decision making.

Skills Used/Demonstrated: I relied heavily on my skills in planning and organization. Problem solving by breaking problems down into smaller steps was frequently used throughout this project. Many issues had quick fixes but there were also those that required a great deal of patience, perseverance, and determination to solve. When working in an area I was not familiar with I did research on the web, with books, as well as examining a working example, and of course, YouTube. I did testing to identify and correct any bugs. I worked closely with the directors - the end users both in training how to use the application and to gather feedback which informed changes to make it more user friendly and to add new functionality.

Lessons Learned: Early in the project it surprised me how differently people interacted with the same screen. This was important for me to remember when designing or making changes - not to assume the users interpretation is always the same as my own. While the database is fully functioning, I believe I would have benefitted from working with an experienced team; allowing for feedback and improved efficiency.

Sponsorship Program

Project Summary: Applying technology to increase sponsorship renewals

Skills Used/Demonstrated: This project is an example of being resourceful and taking initiative as this was not something I was assigned or asked to do but rather a proposal I brought to the team. I came across these Adobe Spark Pages which make creating a very polished presentation quick and easy (and free). I combined this resource with our email client service and created an auto send to sponsors in their final month of sponsorship.

Lessons Learned: It is always worth it to keep an eye on new apps and technology. This was a fairly quick project to create and it runs on its own all year.
